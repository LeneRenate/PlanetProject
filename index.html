The Solar System

The Solar System is made up of the Sun and everything that orbits around it, including the eight planets, their moons, dwarf planets, asteroids, and comets. The Sun's gravity holds it all together, making it the center of the system.

Terrestrial Planets

Closest to the Sun are the small rocky planets like Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars, while farther out lie the gas and ice giants—Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Beyond them stretch regions filled with icy bodies, such as the Kuiper Belt and the distant Oort Cloud.
