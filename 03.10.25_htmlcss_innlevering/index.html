The Solar System

The Solar System is made up of the Sun and everything that orbits around it, including the eight planets, their moons, dwarf planets, asteroids, and comets. The Sun's gravity holds it all together, making it the center of the system. Closest to the Sun are the small rocky planets like Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars, while farther out lie the gas and ice giantsâ€”Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Beyond them stretch regions filled with icy bodies, such as the Kuiper Belt and the distant Oort Cloud.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with a mass nearly 2.5 times that of all the other planets in the Solar System combined and slightly less than one-thousandth the mass of the Sun.
